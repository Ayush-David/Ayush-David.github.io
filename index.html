Research Vision

I build perception systems that work at the boundary between laboratory and deployment - where sensor noise, compute budgets, and real-world variance conspire against benchmark accuracy.

The common thread: making inference both fast enough to deploy and honest enough to trust.

  1. Satellite Image Intelligence physics-constrained super-resolution for thermal and optical imagery; self-supervised training without HR ground truth (Landsat 8, ISRO data pipelines).
  2. Edge-Deployed Video Analytics real-time CCTV forensics with semantic-first retrieval; YOLO/InsightFace/VLM inference stacks on Jetson hardware (ARTPARK @ IISc).
  3. Autonomous Systems Perception HIL test benches, sensor fusion transformers, and CARLA/Autoware AV stack integration.

Technical Reports

  1. Dual Image Super-Resolution and Blind Image Quality Assessment for Satellite Imagery
    Ayush R David · ISRO BAH 2025 Technical Report · July 2025 · [PDF — coming soon]
    Abstract ▾

    Dual-capture SR pipeline achieving 41.45 dB PSNR / 0.97 SSIM on the official ISRO test set, with a ViT+ResNet50 BIQA system trained on 17,000+ image pairs.

  2. Physics-Constrained Thermal Super-Resolution with Causal Optical Guidance
    Ayush R David · SIH 2025 Technical Report · Jan 2025 · [PDF — coming soon]
    Abstract ▾

    Self-supervised 2×–4× thermal SR using gated FiLM fusion and a differentiable Planck law forward model, evaluated on RMSE(K), PSNR, and SSIM without HR ground truth.

  3. Real-Time Semantic CCTV Analytics with Air-Gapped LLM Orchestration
    Ayush R David · ARTPARK @ IISc Internal Report · 2025 · [PDF — coming soon]
    Abstract ▾

    Production platform combining Kafka-based streaming, multi-model inference (YOLO, InsightFace, VLMs), and RAG over Qdrant for natural language forensic queries on live video.
